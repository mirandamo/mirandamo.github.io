IvyHacks 2020

Context

Duration: Jul - Oct 2020

With millions of students stuck at home, 6 Ivy Leagues took this opportunity to co-host the ultimate virtual hackathon. In 4 days, IvyHacks welcomed 3000+ students around the world to unite and solve global problems using technology.

Problem

How do we foster collaboration and learning in an isolated (virtual) setting with a non-elitist image?

Solution

We decided on the theme of Unity and Growth. Unity is connectedness during an isolated period, bringing people together, and growth stems from the word ‚Äúivy‚Äù and how they build off of each other to grow higher.

Ideation Process

To let students experience a rewarding hackathon just from their laptops, we kept the above goals in mind during our visual identity brainstorming process.

IvyHacks Final Design

After rounds of changes, I transformed our ideas into 3D designs. As I was the most skilled in 3D software, I modeled all of the designs while helping others develop sketches based on our theme.

Impact

In the end, the hackathon was a success!

Reflection

It was truly an astounding experience working with talented students from other schools. My biggest takeaways:

  • Be ambitious with ideas, but scale down during execution. I overestimated the team's energy and time commitment, so we needed to simplify a lot of the final designs in the end.
  • Think ahead and "assembly line" simple social media posts. I made most of the 3D assets reusable pngs so the marketing members can easily create new posts.
  • Big events are hard to manage. Even with a style guide, not all posts are perfectly coherent. Don't be too hard on yourself - reinforce standards with people who are not careful, and do better on the next post.

Hack@Brown 2020

Context

Duration: Sep 2019 - Jan 2020

Hack@Brown is the annual hackathon run by Brown University and Rhode Island School of Design students. Through a friendly visual identity, we want to combat the stereotype of people being in hackathons for the prize than the experience.

Problem

How do we foster collaboration and learning in an isolated (virtual) setting with a non-elitist image?

Solution

We decided on the theme of Unity and Growth. Unity is connectedness during an isolated period, bringing people together, and growth stems from the word ‚Äúivy‚Äù and how they build off of each other to grow higher.

H@B Final Design

3 designers, including myself, were responsible for designing the registration pages and dashboard. Specifically, I was in charge of the registration‚Äôs UIUX and planned the motion graphics for each screen, myself animating the books falling.

Challenges

1. Diverging from the landing page's vertical direction

Because it was hard to implement a vertically jumping page, we added animations every time the user clicks next to designed the registration form to move horizontally to seem like one story.

2. Inclusive design for underrepresented group

I researched multiple gender/race inclusive surveys, including the US Race and Ethnicity Census to achieve appropriate content. Implementing dropdown multi-select was time consuming for the Dev team, but I advocated for inclusivity and its relation to Hack@Brown's values.

Reflection

In the end, the hackathon was a success! We had more than 400 participants and 1500 unique users at peak on our website. It was a truly humbling experience and I loved my team!

To improve our designs and brand awareness for next year, I will:

  • Storyboard the user journey to improve UX flow
  • Adapt 3D renderings onto products - tote bags, sweatshirts, etc.
  • Identify goals to motivate entire design process
