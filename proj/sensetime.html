Overview

During my 6 month internship at SenseTime, I was the UX Design intern for SenseTime in Shanghai, China. SenseTime is an AI unicorn specializing with a current valuation of $4.5 billion.

As part of the User Experience Design team, I created the UI/UX for their smart community web app SenseSpace and developed 3D brand characters for their Education Line website.

Real Estate: SenseSpace

What I Did

While I can't disclose all the project details due to NDA, feel free to reach out to me and I would love to talk more about what I did!

I was responsible for the interaction design of SenseSpace, a smart community web app connected to facial recognition cameras to improve the security in residential compounds. It is purchased by the Sichuan provincial government to be implemented across the province.

At a higher level, I worked on defining the problem, mapping out the user journey, iteratively prototyping lo-fi and hi-fi demos, considering engineering constraints, analyzing competitors, and keeping new functionalities aligned to the design system. For now, you can see the login page I redesigned!

Learnings from UX Design

Working cross-functionally with PMs, Engineers, and Designers, I grew immensely as a designer and team-player. This project also taught me how to resolve many UX problems.

1. Accessibility for seniors

Since our main users are security guards over 50 years old, I learned to prioritize simple interactions and clarity over aesthetics.

2. Lack of user research

Oftentimes, we are adding/changing functionalities without user testing data from released versions. Therefore, we had to be creative in how to verify our users' needs. I learned to spend time digging through online forums and news to get a sense of what problems the users are facing.

3. Inconsistent user needs

We are humans and what we say isn't always what we want. So each week we are faced with new problems. Instead of creating new designs to solve new problems, we should maintain a holistic view and approach new problems with existing interaction designs in mind.

Learnings from Visual Design

1. UI trends might not be user-friendly.

I explored Neumorphic UI elements, whose realism is more coherent with 3D design. However, its use of low contrast makes it hard for visually impaired people to see.

2. Design for developers.

I wrote out the exact CSS animation code for the landing page redesign, and the engineers were grateful that we were communicating with the same jargon.
