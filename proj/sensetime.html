SenseSpace

What I Did

I was responsible for the interaction design of SenseSpace, a smart community web app connected to AI facial recognition security cameras in residential compounds. It is purchased by the Sichuan provincial government to be implemented across 100 compounds in 2020. At a higher level, I worked on defining the problem, analyzing competitors, and iteratively prototyping lo-fi and hi-fi demos.

Problem

How do we improve the security of residential compounds with AI cameras?

Solution

Create a multi-functional web app where cameras flag suspicious people and notify the guards. Property managers can also access the cameras through our provided mobile app that better fits their on-the-go life.

While I can't disclose all the project details due to NDA, feel free to reach out to me and I would love to talk more about what I did!

SenseStudy

What I Did

Another project that I worked on SenseTime's education platform SenseStudy. I developed 3D IP characters and a UI style guide for the official website. It is visited by 135K+ students and 250+ schools. Furthermore, I adapted my renders into the login and explore page redesigns.

Problem

How do we represent software and hardware development through our visual identity and distinguish ourselves from competitive products?

Solution

Redesign the IP characters in 3D to make the learning platform more interactive and engaging.

3D IP Character Design

Based on the existing 2D line designs, I developed 5 characters from scratch. It was my most challenging 3D design project and a strenuous process of modeling, shading, rigging, skinning, and rendering.

SenseInnoLab Explore Page Redesign

Using the 3D characters that I modeled, I redesigned the lab component's explore page. This product teaches K-12 students software development for AI sensor hardware in their IT classes, so I also created reusable 3D assets related to sensors and cameras.

Takeaways

Working cross-functionally with PMs, Engineers, and Designers, I grew immensely as a designer and team-player. This project also taught me how to resolve many UX and visual design problems.

1. Accessibility for an aging population

Since the smart community web app's main users are security guards over 50 years old, I learned to prioritize simple interactions and clarity over visual design.

2. UI trends might not be user-friendly

I initially designed a Neumorphic UI system for the education platform, because its realism is more coherent with 3D design. However, after doing more research on the topic, I learned that its use of low color contrast makes it hard for visually-impaired people to see. I archived my old document and stuck with 2D UI designs to ensure that our platform is all-inclusive.

3. Inconsistent user needs

We are humans and what we say isn't always what we want. Each week, we are faced with new problems on how to design the experience of a new functionality. Instead of creating new designs to solve new problems, we should maintain a holistic view and approach new problems with existing interaction designs in mind.
